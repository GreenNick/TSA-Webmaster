Our TSA Chapter

The TSA program at Tucker High School is an organized, student-run club in which students have the opportunity to explore different technological and engineering topics. They can choose to investigate many different areas of technology based on their interests and passions.

How We're Organized

Tucker TSA is organized in a way that everyone is involved and there is always work being done. We have an outline of priorities and activities for the year. For example, specific goals, and deadlines specific to their events. A program of work is necessary for us in order to guide members in an organized and proficient manner. It also keeps a log and reflects on what needs to be done and when it needs to be done by.

What We Do

Hold Chapter Meetings

  • Record the date of all planned chapter meetings
  • Prior to each meeting, create an agenda of all topics that will be covered
  • Creating an agenda will minimize any surprises that may come up at meetings

Recruit Members

  • Gain back more members than we lost the past year
  • Expose more people to technological careers through TSA and help build leadership skills
  • Create connections with professionals and peers

Keep Track of Expenses

  • Keeping track of expenses is imperative to chapter success
  • Make sure to meet your school’s accountant to get a better understanding of all possible expenses.
  • Any costs for fundraising
  • Materials for events
  • Hotels for overnight trips
  • Buses

Fundraising

The officers of Tucker TSA come up with and implement a plethora of various fundraising ideas such as the following:

  • Game night
  • Selling food
  • Selling products from companies
  • Local restaurants that give a percentage of sales for a certain night
  • Raffles
  • Custom apparel sales
  • Car Washes

Conduct Officer Meetings

Things to Remember for Officer Meetings:

  • Have something to write ideas on
  • Focus on events in order by deadlines
  • Create ways to communicate easier
  • Meetings should be weekly to every other week
  • Make meetings more frequent closer to conferences
  • Practice parliamentary procedure

Reflect on Past Conferences

  • It is important to reflect on how your chapter did after conferences to determine your accomplishments.
  • Take time after conferences to ask about the experiences in the conference.
  • How did they do?
  • What did they enjoy?
  • What didn’t they enjoy?
  • What could be improved?
